
# AI Design Failure Patterns

Common mistakes produced by AI design tools and generators.

Recognizing these patterns helps diagnose problems quickly.

---

## Gradient Hero Syndrome

Large hero section with:

- big gradient background
- oversized headline
- vague tagline
- generic call‑to‑action

Problem:
Visually impressive but communicates nothing specific.

Fix:
Replace vague messaging with clear value proposition.

---

## Card Explosion

Page filled with dozens of cards.

Symptoms:

- too many containers
- equal visual weight
- no hierarchy

Problem:
Everything competes for attention.

Fix:
Group related content and create hierarchy.

---

## Floating CTA

Call‑to‑action appears visually disconnected from context.

Symptoms:

- CTA randomly placed
- weak narrative flow

Fix:
Place CTA immediately after persuasive content.

---

## Typography Scale Drift

Typography sizes appear inconsistent.

Symptoms:

- headings randomly sized
- body text inconsistent

Fix:
Define a clear type scale.

Example:

H1
H2
H3
Body
Caption

---

## Decorative Overload

Too many visual effects.

Symptoms:

- gradients
- shadows
- glow effects
- icons everywhere

Problem:
Visual noise reduces clarity.

Fix:
Reduce decorative elements and emphasize hierarchy.

---

## Section Duplication

Multiple sections communicate the same message.

Symptoms:

- repeated feature lists
- redundant testimonials
- repeated benefits

Fix:
Merge redundant sections.

---

## Polished but Empty

AI designs often look good but lack strategic clarity.

Symptoms:

- attractive layout
- unclear value proposition

Fix:
Strengthen messaging before visual design.

---

## Misaligned Grids

Elements appear visually misaligned.

Symptoms:

- inconsistent spacing
- edges not aligned

Fix:
Adopt a consistent grid system.

---

## CTA Overcrowding

Too many buttons competing.

Symptoms:

- multiple primary CTAs
- equal visual weight

Fix:
Define a single primary action.

---

## Fake Trust Signals

AI sometimes invents credibility signals.

Examples:

- fake testimonials
- invented company logos

Fix:
Only include real trust indicators.
